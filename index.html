Personas and Storyboard LALALA

Section 1: Interface sketch

The interface I chose is the digital menu of a restaurant nearby. The first screen the main page of the menu, which includes menu items with images. Clicking onto the image leads to a second screen, where users can see a larger image and detailed descriptions.

Section 2: User research

Interview questions:

  1. What prior knowledge do you have of the interface?
  2. What goal are you trying to achieve in the interaction with this interface? Do you have a clear idea of what you want or figure out as you browse through?
  3. What aspects of the interface stand out the most and help you decide on what you want? Name? Description? Image? Price?
  4. How do you go through the interface? Go through everything? Go through by categories?
  5. Did you know that you can click on the image for a description?
  6. Do you feel the interface is overall easy to navigate? On a scale of 0-5.
  7. Do you feel confused by any part of the interface? Any non-descriptive text/images? Unnatural visual elements (color, font, spacing, etc.)?
Key obervations Interview responses
1 He scrolled down the menu slowly and carefully, taking enough time to look at each item. He looked frustrated and pauses multiple times when encountering an unfamiliar dish, perhaps looking up something that he don't understand with his phone. He was the only responder that clicked on the items for an enlarged view of the image and further description. He scrolled up and down multiple times to confirm the dish he'd like to get.
  • First time to the restaurant under a friend's recommendation, has almost no knowledge of the cuisine and dishes
  • Looks through the menu carefully, especially at the images
  • Tries to look up some dish names but found the results unhelpful, decided mainly based on the images
  • Clicks into the image for a larger view, but isn't aware of the detailed description
  • Provides an overall score of 3.5/5 to the navigability of the interface, points deducted mainly for the confusing "Buy Now" button
2 First, he quickly scrolled through the menu, speaking to himself as he looks at the subsection names. He skipped parts of the menu, looked carefully at the main entree section, and pondered a while to decide on what to get. He tried to click on the "Buy Now" button but nothing happens, and he frowned.
  • Has never been to the restaurant before, but knows about the cuisine
  • Has a preliminary knowledge of what to expect on the menu, so skips through sections that don't interest him (such as appetizers)
  • The name and the image help him decide on what to get
  • Isn't aware of the "category" option or the function of clicking on the images for detailed descriptions, but also claimed he wouldn't use them whatsoever
  • Provides an overall score of 3.5/5 on the navigability of the interface, but doesn't provide a specific reason
3 He scrolled down to an entree on the menu directly, as if he were very familiar with the dish and its location on the menu. Then, he returned to the top and recommends some dishes to his friend as he go through the menu again slowly and carefully. When his friend asked about the "Buy Now" option, he told his friend that it was probably not working, but still showed it to his friend.
  • Has been to the restaurant frequently, and clearly knows what wants to get
  • Jumps directly to the dish he wants when he orders for himself, and moves slower as he explains to his friend
  • Prior knowledge helps him decide on what to get
  • Isn't aware of the "category" function or the "further details" function, believes these two functions are unnecessary because the images are self-explanatory enough
  • Provides an overall score of 4/5 to the navigability of the interface, commends on the images
